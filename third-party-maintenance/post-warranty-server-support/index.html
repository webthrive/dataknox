What Happens the Day After Warranty Expires?

When a server's OEM warranty expires, most IT teams face an uncomfortable choice: auto-renew at escalating OEM prices, or go without coverage. Dataknox provides a third path — post-warranty server support that matches OEM SLAs at 30–85% less. No coverage gap, no rushed refresh, no OEM dependency.

When It Makes Sense

When Does Post-Warranty Support Make Sense?

Aging Server Fleet

Your servers are 3–5+ years old and OEM renewal pricing has climbed well beyond what the hardware risk justifies.

Budget Pressure

IT budget is constrained and OEM maintenance is one of the largest controllable line items in the infrastructure budget.

EoL Approaching

OEM end-of-life is declared but a hardware refresh isn't in the near-term plan — you need coverage continuity.

Refresh Delay

Capital budget for refresh was deferred. You need 12–36 more months of reliable coverage on existing hardware.

Mixed-OEM Environment

Multiple OEM brands are up for renewal at different times. A single TPM contract simplifies procurement and billing.

M&A Hardware Consolidation

Post-acquisition hardware estates with overlapping OEM contracts are a prime candidate for TPM consolidation.
